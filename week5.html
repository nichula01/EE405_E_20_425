Week 5 Progress: Non-Linear Unmixing Methods

This week focused on understanding non-linear spectral mixing models used in hyperspectral unmixing.

Overview of Non-Linear Mixing

  • Unlike linear models, non-linear models capture multiple scattering and intimate mixing effects.
  • Essential for complex scenes like vegetation canopies, urban areas, and mineral mixtures.

Techniques Explored

  • Kernel-Based Models: Used kernel trick to model non-linear mixing in a high-dimensional space.
  • Bilinear Models: Considered interactions between endmembers (e.g., GBM, PPNM).
  • Neural Network Models: Introduced MLP-based unmixing frameworks for learning complex mappings.

Key Learnings

  • Non-linear models improve accuracy in real-world complex environments.
  • They often require more data and computational resources.
  • Trade-off exists between model complexity and interpretability.

    Parallel to hyperspectral unmixing studies, working on a practical application of remote sensing for urban analysis.

    Project Objective

    Developing a deep learning model to detect and classify slum vs non-slum regions in Colombo using both RGB and Sentinel-2 multispectral imagery. The goal is to compare the effectiveness of RGB-only versus multispectral approaches for urban slum identification.

    Data Sources & Methodology

    • Training Data: Mumbai and Caracas labeled datasets for initial model development
    • Target Area: Colombo city - manual annotation using high-resolution satellite imagery
    • Ground Truth Creation: Using Google Street View for validation as suggested by supervisor
    • Multispectral Data: Sentinel-2 L2A products for enhanced spectral classification
    • Approach: 256x256 tile-based training and comparison of RGB vs MSI performance

    Expected Outcomes

    • Quantitative comparison between RGB and multispectral approaches
    • Model generalization assessment across different urban environments
    • Ground truth validation using Street View imagery
    • Practical tool for urban planning and slum mapping in Colombo
