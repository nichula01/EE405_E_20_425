Week 2 Progress: Sentinel-2 Data Exploration

This week focused on accessing, understanding, and processing Sentinel-2 satellite imagery for Sri Lanka through the Copernicus Open Access Hub.

Copernicus Hub Exploration

Successfully explored the Copernicus Open Access Hub platform to search, filter, and download satellite imagery.

  • Created a Copernicus account and learned the navigation interface
  • Set up search filters to focus on Sri Lanka geographical area
  • Applied filter parameters for cloud coverage, acquisition dates, and sensing orbit
  • Downloaded multiple Sentinel-2 Level-2A products over key regions of interest
  • Organized downloaded data into a structured project repository

Sentinel-2 Data & Band Analysis

Studied the structure and characteristics of Sentinel-2 multispectral imagery.

  • Analyzed the 13 spectral bands of Sentinel-2 and their specific applications
  • Compared the different spatial resolutions (10m, 20m, 60m) of various bands
  • Studied the metadata included in Sentinel-2 products
  • Learned about Level-1C (Top of Atmosphere) vs. Level-2A (Bottom of Atmosphere) products
  • Examined the spectral response functions for each band

Research Paper Reviewed: Hyperspectral Unmixing

This week, I reviewed an in-depth research paper focused on hyperspectral unmixing techniques combining image processing and machine learning strategies. The paper outlines both conventional and advanced unmixing methods and introduces an open-source Python package named HySUPP that supports supervised, semi-supervised, and blind (unsupervised) unmixing approaches.

Key Takeaways

  • Overview of linear vs. nonlinear mixing models in hyperspectral imaging
  • Introduction of the HySUPP Python package supporting reproducible benchmarking
  • Comparative analysis of various unmixing methods using synthetic and real-world datasets
  • Discussion on deep learning-based autoencoder architectures for abundance estimation
  • Insight into spectral variability, noise, and preprocessing challenges
