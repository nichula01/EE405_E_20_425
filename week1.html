Background Learning

Understanding the fundamentals of different image types and their applications.

  • Pixels, RGB, Multispectral (MSI), and Hyperspectral (HSI) imaging concepts
  • Spectral resolution comparisons between RGB, MSI, and HSI
  • Number of bands and their significance in different imaging techniques
Hyperspectral and Multispectral Imaging What is hyperspectral imaging

Applications of HSI

Exploring real-world use cases of hyperspectral imaging technology.

  • Agriculture - crop health monitoring and soil analysis
  • Environmental monitoring - pollution detection and ecosystem analysis
  • Military surveillance - target detection and camouflage identification
  • Medical imaging - disease detection and tissue analysis
Hyperspectral remote sensing and its applications

Hyperspectral Unmixing

Learning how to decompose mixed spectral signatures into their pure components.

  • Spectral unmixing fundamental concepts
  • Endmembers - pure spectral signatures in the scene
  • Abundances - proportion of each endmember in a pixel
Recent Advances in Spectral Unmixing for Hyperspectral Imagery

Unsupervised Learning

Understanding algorithms that can identify patterns without labeled data.

  • Clustering techniques (K-means, hierarchical clustering)
  • Principal Component Analysis (PCA) for dimensionality reduction
  • Autoencoders for unsupervised feature extraction from hyperspectral data
What is Unsupervised Learning

Mathematical Models

Exploring mathematical frameworks for describing spectral mixing processes.

  • Linear Mixture Model (LMM) - simplest and widely used approach
  • Nonlinear Mixture Models (NLMM) for complex interactions
  • Bilinear and Polynomial Models for multiple scattering
  • Multiple Scattering Models for advanced applications

Endmember & Abundance Estimation

Learning methods to extract pure spectral signatures and their proportions.

  • Techniques for endmember extraction from hyperspectral images
  • Creation of abundance maps showing material distribution
  • Comparison between ground truth and abundance predictions
